To illustrate the computation of the relative degree of competition (Ci(j)) using Equation (2), consider the relationship between Apple and Samsung. As shown in Table 3, these two brands first appeared together in the cell highlighted in green as a competitive cluster when the topic count was reduced from 8 to 5, which resulted in tAS = 5. Samsung was the first competitor to appear alongside Apple, which had not previously belonged to any competitive cluster until the topic count was reduced to 5 (Apple appeared alone in higher topic counts as shown in the red-highlighted cells). This result indicates that Apple had no competitors included in any cluster prior to this point, making Samsung its first competitor, resulting in a rank value of rA(S) = 1. Furthermore, in this competitive cluster, only two brands (Apple and Samsung) were present, so nAS = 0. Based on the formula, Apple’s relative degree of competition with Samsung was thus calculated as CA(S) = tAS − rA(S) − nAS = 5 − 1 − 0 = 4. Similarly, competition scores with other brands were computed following the same procedure.

As a demonstration of the relative degree of competition computation, let us examine the relationship between Apple and Xiaomi. According to Table 4, these two brands first co-occurred in a competitive cluster when the topic count was reduced from 9 to 6 (cell highlighted in green), which sets tAX = 6. Apple's competition with any other competitor did not appear until the topic count reached 6, implying that Xiaomi is its first competitor (as shown in the red-highlighted cells, Apple appears without any competitor before the topic count 6); therefore, the ranking value rA(X) = 1 is assigned. In this joint cluster (highlighted in green), there was one additional brand, OPPO/vivo, so nAX = 1. Applying the formula, Apple’s relative degree of competition with Xiaomi is calculated as CA(X) = tAX − rA(X) − nAX = 6 − 1 − 1 = 4.

From Xiaomi’s perspective, Apple appears as the second competitor, following OPPO/vivo (as shown in the blue-highlighted cells, marked only for Xiaomi’s presence with topic counts above 6, Xiaomi co-occurs with a single competitor, OPPO/vivo, at topic count 8 and 7), assigning a rank value rX(A) = 2. As already found with nAX = 1, their shared cluster includes one additional brand, OPPO/vivo (colored in green), thus nXA = nAX = 1. Therefore, Xiaomi’s relative degree of competition with Apple is given by CX(A) = tXA − rX(A) − nXA = 6 − 2 − 1 = 3, highlighting an asymmetric competitive relationship.
